Concrete Mix Calculator - Get the Right Blend

Standard concrete uses a 1:2:3 ratio. That's 1 part cement, 2 parts sand, 3 parts gravel.

For one cubic yard, you need:

  • Cement: 6-7 bags (94 pounds each)
  • Sand: 1,400 pounds
  • Gravel: 1,900 pounds
  • Water: 35 gallons

Professional Concrete Estimator Tips

After 15 years in construction, here's what I've learned:

Always add waste factor. I use 10% for simple slabs. Complex shapes get 15%. You can't return concrete once it's mixed.

Weather affects timing. Hot days make concrete set faster. Cold days slow it down. Plan your pour time carefully.

Prepare everything first. Have tools ready. Level the ground. Set forms properly. Concrete trucks don't wait.

Order morning delivery. Concrete is easier to work when it's cooler. Afternoon sun makes finishing harder.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate concrete for a 10x10 slab?

Multiply 10 × 10 × thickness in feet. For 4-inch thick: 10 × 10 × 0.33 = 33 cubic feet. Divide by 27 = 1.22 cubic yards. Add 10% waste = 1.34 cubic yards total.

What's the concrete calculator formula?

Length × Width × Thickness (all in feet) ÷ 27 = cubic yards needed. Always add 10-15% for waste factor.

How many 80lb bags of concrete make a yard?

You need 45 bags of 80-pound concrete mix to make one cubic yard. For smaller projects, this is often cheaper than ready mix delivery.

Can I use this concrete estimator for stamped concrete?

Yes, stamped concrete uses the same volume calculations. The calculator works for all concrete types including decorative, stamped, and colored concrete.

What thickness should my concrete be?

Driveways need 5-6 inches. Patios need 4 inches. Footings need 8-12 inches. Sidewalks need 4 inches. The calculator suggests thickness based on project type.

How much does concrete cost per cubic yard?

Ready mix concrete costs $100-150 per cubic yard depending on your location. Our concrete cost calculator uses current regional pricing.

Do I need to include rebar in concrete calculations?

Rebar takes up minimal space. The standard 10% waste factor covers rebar volume. No need for separate calculations.

How far in advance should I order concrete?

Order ready mix concrete 2-3 days ahead. Schedule morning delivery when possible. Concrete must be poured within 90 minutes of mixing.

Can the calculator handle irregular shapes?

Break irregular areas into rectangles and triangles. Calculate each section separately. Add the results together for total volume needed.

What's the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is one ingredient in concrete. Concrete is cement mixed with sand, gravel, and water. The calculator figures out all ingredients needed.

How do I calculate concrete for stairs?

Measure each step separately. Calculate the volume of each tread and riser. Add them together. The concrete calculator has a stairs option that does this automatically.

What happens if I order too much concrete?

Extra concrete can't be returned. You'll pay for disposal. That's why accurate calculation matters. Use our calculator to avoid overordering.

Can I calculate concrete for a round patio?

Yes. Enter the diameter and thickness. The calculator uses the circle formula (π × radius²) to find the area, then calculates volume.

How much water do I add to concrete mix?

The concrete mix calculator shows water amounts. Generally use 5-6 gallons per 94-pound bag of cement. Too much water weakens concrete.

What's a concrete waste factor?

The waste factor accounts for spillage, uneven ground, and small calculation errors. Always include 10% minimum. Complex projects need 15%.

How many wheelbarrows equal a cubic yard?

A standard wheelbarrow holds about 6 cubic feet. One cubic yard needs 4-5 wheelbarrow loads. Plan your crew size accordingly.
